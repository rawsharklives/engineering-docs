---
title: "Root Cause Analyses"
description: "Blameless incident retrospectives identifying root causes, contributing factors, and action items."
---

Root Cause Analyses (RCAs) are blameless. Their purpose is to understand what happened,
why it happened, and what systemic changes will prevent recurrence. They are not for
assigning fault.

Incidents rarely have a single root cause. An RCA should identify all contributing root
causes — technical, process, and human factors — rather than stopping at the first
explanation found. Addressing only one cause while others remain leaves the system
vulnerable to recurrence.

See `templates/rca-template.md` for the standard format.
